BenchBuddy

Wearable sensors that automatically log a user's weight lifting workout.

These sensors track the weight lifting exercises you do so you don't have to keep manual logs.
The sensors record and transmit motion data via bluetooth to a connected iOS device.
A sensor unit is strapped to the wearer's wrists.
While the user works lifts weights (bench press, bicep curls, etc.) the devices capture motion data.
Collected accelerometer/gyroscope data is processed on an iOS device which implements a trained CLF Decision Tree used for activity recognition.
  • For: 6.s062 Mobile and Sensor Computing
  • Date: Spring 2016
  • Skills: ArduinoC, iOS, Machine Learning, Signal Processing

WW4Net

Convolutional neural network for scene recognition.

WW4Net is based on the 16 layer version of VGGNet, one of the convolutional neural networks which most successfully classifies the ImageNet database. The adaptations my teammate and I designed make WW4Net suitable for scene classification in MIT's Mini Places Challenge. Our main contribution is the tactical reduction of the VGG-16 ConvNet such that it does not overfit when trained on the Mini Places database, which is substantially smaller than ImageNet. By employing multi-cropping techniques and combining the classifications of separately trained models at test time, we achieve a 45.3% top-1 error and a 18.43% top-5 error. These results secured our teamâ€™s first place position in the Mini Places 2015 Challenge. Over 20 teams competed, most of which were comprised of graduate students.

  • Date: December 2015
  • Skills: Neural Networks, Caffe, PyCaffe

Solar Sprint

Solar Sprint is an iOS game written in Objective-C using the Cocos2D framework. In January 2014, I entered the game into MIT's iOS Game Development Competition, where it took the Grand Prize. Writing the game was an exercise in managing a large base of software between myself and my two teammates. I wrote our own physics engine to support the simplified and specific kinematics of the game, and also wrote a system that allowed us to easily transcribe different paths of planetary motion into the XML files which are loaded as levels. Read more about the specifics of the software implementation here.

  • For: MIT iOS Game Competition
  • Date: January 2014
  • Skills: iOS Development, Game Design

Fabrication by Example

Allows novices to design new & complex 3D models based on a database of pre-defined parts.

Load an old model and adjust its parameters (ie. translate, scale, rotate).
As you combine components, the software automatically adds connective parts (screws, hinges, etc.) in the necessary quantities and locations.
Swap analagous elements. New glass table top "snaps" to fit the tables dimensions.
Easily customize simple shelving set by adding a cabinet door, which automatically scales to the shelf dimensions.
Turn the shelving unit into a desk by adding table top and legs modules.
Once you finish your design, the software tells you exactly what materials you need so building your design is straight forward!

Acepted to SIGGRAPH 2014 and featured on MIT CSAIL's news site!

  • For: CFG / MIT CSAIL
  • Date: June 2013 - January 2014
  • Skills: C++, Cinder, computer graphics

Climbing Wheel

Just for kicks, a friend and I built a rotating rock climbing wall.
The structure is entirely made out of wood, except for the axle (steel pipe) and bearings.
It took six days and the labor of ~20 new freshmen to construct.
Individual rings for the "ribcage" of the wheel were constructed on the ground and then lifted onto the axle.
After surfacing the skeletal structure with 1/2" plywood, we attached rock holds.
  • For: East Campus Rush
  • Date: August 2013
  • Skills: Woodworking

Fearkat

The Fearkat is the world's first laser tag game embedded in a huggable stuffed animal.
Tiny infrared transmitters and receivers placed in the plushie's head act as the basis for the tagging system.
When the tail is lowered, the stuffed animal enters laser mode.
A 3D printed trigger mechanism is placed in the tail.
The tail-trigger mechanism is simultaneously cuddly and ergonomic.
Electronics backing the LED's, sensors, and trigger are tucked away in the body, masked by fluff.
The Fearkat's eyes and heart light up in response to firing a shot or being fired at.

Read more about the toy here.
